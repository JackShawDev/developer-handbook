# End of Day Reflection

I feel like I'm gaining a deeper understanding of Python by taking a slow iterative approach to learning, when trying to learn Python in school or through time limited training programs they tend to rush content before I fully grasp the content. For example they would attempt to cover 5-6 data types in one lesson but leave out explanations of how operators interact between variables of either matching or differing data types. This means I'm always left with a solid understanding and itching to learn more rather than being overwhelmed and told to learn the rest outside of a lesson without guidance on where to start. As the content becomes more challenging I can see myself hitting a mental block in the future but this is normal for learning anything.
